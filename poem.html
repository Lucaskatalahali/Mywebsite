The Pattern of Beauty

Where is the pattern of what is beautiful,
If not in the sound that rises from the perpendicularity of birds chirping,
And the wind shaking the leaves and trunks
On which they are singing.

Where is pure beauty,
If not in the blooming of each flower at dawn,
In the sunset and its sunrise,
The exact moment when the mountain sees it kiss the sea,
And the plateau contemplates its dawn.

What will beauty be,
If not the sound that arises from the intersection of wings beating in the air
And the propulsion of the fin through the sea.

Where does pure beauty hover,
If not in the spontaneous smile of a child,
And in the tears of a mother when, from herself, she sees them being born?
Here lies the full definition of beauty:
Nature smiling at you.
Author: Lucas I. C. Katalahali
